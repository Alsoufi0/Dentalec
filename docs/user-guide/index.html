1

Create an account or sign in

Your private workspace keeps your sources, notes, flashcards, and progress saved.

How to use

  1. Open the app. New here? Click Create an account.
  2. Enter your name, email, and a password (at least 8 characters).
  3. Returning? Switch to Sign in and enter your email and password.
  4. Use the Light / Dark toggle (top right) any time.
Private by design: everything you upload stays inside your own account.
2

Add your study source

Everything the app generates is built only from the material you add here.

How to use

  1. Go to Notes & Books in the sidebar.
  2. Drop a PDF (lecture, chapter, handout) into the upload box, or paste notes into the text box and press Index.
  3. Wait a few seconds while it indexes — it then appears under Indexed material.
  4. Remove any source with the trash icon next to it.
Start here. Most features stay locked until you add at least one source.
3

The Dashboard

Your home base: jump back in, explore anatomy, and see your progress.

What you see

  1. Continue studying picks up where you left off.
  2. Interactive Tooth Anatomy — tap any layer (enamel, dentin, pulp…) to study it with an accurate label and notes.
  3. Your Progress shows overall readiness and a topic mastery map.
  4. The left sidebar is how you move between every tool.
Tip: the app is bounded like a real app — content scrolls inside the panel, so pages never run on endlessly.
4

AI Tutor — ask anything

Ask a focused question and get a clear, source-grounded answer.

How to use

  1. Open AI Tutor.
  2. Type a question in the box at the bottom, or tap a suggested question.
  3. Read the answer — it is organised into clear sections grounded in your notes.
  4. Use Listen to hear it, or Cards to turn it into flashcards.
The AI Tutor is the one place that can run long — it is a conversation. Everywhere else stays compact.
5

Study Tools — one focused output

Turn your source into a specific study artifact. Pick the way you want to study.

How to use

  1. Open Study Tools.
  2. From the Way of study dropdown, choose what you need — gap check, differential table, visual map, mnemonics, and more.
  3. Press Generate. Each choice produces a different, on-topic result.
Each tool is unique: a differential gives a comparison table, visual learning gives a step map, and so on.
6

How answers are shown

Long content is interactive and scannable — never a wall of text.

What to expect

  1. Comparison tables are clean and condensed for quick scanning.
  2. Long answers split into collapsible sections — open just what you need.
  3. Processes render as a step map with connected nodes.
  4. Wide tables scroll sideways inside their card.
Honesty: answers are concise on purpose, and can still be wrong — check them against your material.
7

Flashcards — active recall

Review one card at a time with spaced repetition.

How to use

  1. Open Flashcards and press Make cards (or use Cards under any tutor answer).
  2. Click the card or press Space to flip and reveal the answer.
  3. Move with Previous / Next (or the ← / → arrow keys).
  4. Rate yourself Again / Hard / Good / Easy to schedule reviews.
Export: download your deck as Anki TSV any time.
8

Quizzes — test yourself

Answer multiple-choice questions and get instant feedback.

How to use

  1. Open Exams (or pick the Examiner tool in Study Tools).
  2. Start the test — questions render as an interactive quiz.
  3. Click an option: the correct answer turns green, a wrong pick turns red, with an explanation.
  4. A running score tracks how many you got right.
Active recall + feedback is the fastest way to find and fix weak spots.
9

Clinical Cases

Practice reasoning with patient scenarios built from your notes.

How to use

  1. Open Clinical Cases.
  2. Pick a format: Clinical case, OSCE station, Exam checklist, or Rescue plan.
  3. Press the card's button to generate it from your source.
  4. Work through the scenario step by step.
OSCE station gives you a patient script plus a marking rubric to rehearse against.
10

Radiology — Case Library & Viewer

Browse X-rays, then read them with real viewer tools.

Case Library

  1. Open Radiology to browse teaching X-rays.
  2. Filter by type, difficulty, or pathology; bookmark with the star.
  3. Upload your own X-ray with the Upload button (delete it any time).
  4. Click a case to open it in the Viewer.
Next slide: the Viewer tools (zoom, contrast, invert, measure, labels).
10b

Radiology — reading a film

Zoom, adjust, measure, and study labelled structures.

Viewer tools

  1. Zoom / pan, adjust brightness, contrast, and invert the greyscale.
  2. Measure length, angle, and area. Use Calibrate (draw a known length) to read in real mm.
  3. Tap a coloured marker to learn that structure; raise the difficulty to reveal subtle findings.
  4. Markers are colour-coded: radiopaque, radiolucent, finding.
Note: labels and measurements are teaching aids and can be approximate — not for diagnosis.
11

X-ray Interpreter

Write your reading of a film and get AI feedback.

How to use

  1. Open X-ray Interpreter and choose a case from the dropdown.
  2. Write your interpretation in the text box.
  3. Press Get feedback — the AI shows what you got right, what you missed, landmarks to look for, and clinical significance, with a score.
  4. Your attempts and scores are tracked.
Honesty: the AI reader can misread radiographs — treat its feedback as a practice partner, never as truth.
12

Handy controls & tips

Small things that make daily studying smoother.

Good to know

  1. Clear (top bar) wipes the conversation and notes but keeps your source and cards.
  2. Delete appears on each message, each uploaded source, and each X-ray you add.
  3. Light / Dark theme toggle is always top-right.
  4. Works on phone and desktop — the layout adapts and never runs off the screen.
Daily AI budget (top-left) shows how many AI actions you have left for the day.

That's the whole app.

The fastest routine: add a source → ask the tutor → make flashcards → quiz yourself → review weak spots. Add radiology practice whenever you want to read films.
